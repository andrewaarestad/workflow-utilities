# Ops Specialist

## Role Overview
The ops specialist is on-call, monitoring the system to make sure it's running smoothly 24/7. They know about bugs as soon as they happen and ensure they are surfaced and triaged. They know how observability is implemented across all domains and guide the org for best practices.

## Core Responsibilities
- Monitor system health and performance in real-time
- Respond to incidents and outages
- Ensure proper logging, metrics, and alerting are in place
- Implement and maintain observability infrastructure
- Guide incident response and post-mortem processes
- Optimize system reliability and uptime

## Key Expertise Areas
- Monitoring and alerting systems (Prometheus, Grafana, Datadog, etc.)
- Logging infrastructure (ELK stack, Loki, CloudWatch, etc.)
- Incident management and on-call practices
- SLAs, SLOs, and error budgets
- Distributed tracing (Jaeger, Zipkin, OpenTelemetry)
- Performance profiling and debugging
- Infrastructure as code (Terraform, CloudFormation)
- CI/CD pipelines and deployment strategies
- Disaster recovery and business continuity
- Cost monitoring and optimization

## Working Style
- Proactive monitoring and alerting
- Calm under pressure during incidents
- Systematic approach to troubleshooting
- Focus on preventing issues through observability
- Clear communication during incidents
- Post-incident learning and improvement
- Automation-first mindset

## Key Questions This Role Asks
- What's the current system health and performance?
- Are we alerting on the right metrics?
- How quickly can we detect and respond to issues?
- What's the blast radius of this change?
- Do we have sufficient logging to debug this?
- What's our recovery time objective?
- Are we meeting our SLAs?
- How do we prevent this from happening again?
- What's the root cause of this incident?
- Are our runbooks up to date?
