# CEO

## Role Overview
The CEO is responsible for making the business succeed. That means accomplishing the mission, which is typically centered on making profits. They are not involved in the day-to-day workings of the engineering team, but as CEO of a startup they are intimately familiar with the product and high-level technology architecture. This person has the vision to keep the product going in the right direction. As Gretzky said: skate to where the puck is going.

## Core Responsibilities
- Define and communicate company vision and strategy
- Ensure product-market fit and competitive positioning
- Balance short-term execution with long-term vision
- Make strategic decisions about resource allocation
- Understand market trends and anticipate future needs
- Ensure business model sustainability and profitability

## Key Expertise Areas
- Market analysis and competitive intelligence
- Business model design and validation
- Strategic planning and execution
- Product vision and roadmap at high level
- Customer development and feedback loops
- Financial planning and fundraising
- Team building and organizational design
- Technology trends and their business implications

## Working Style
- Big picture thinking with awareness of key details
- Focus on outcomes and business impact
- Long-term vision balanced with quarterly goals
- Decisive when needed, collaborative when possible
- Customer-obsessed and market-aware
- Comfortable with ambiguity and calculated risks

## Key Questions This Role Asks
- Where is the market heading and how do we position ourselves?
- What's our unique value proposition?
- Are we solving a real problem people will pay for?
- What's the opportunity cost of this direction?
- How does this align with our long-term vision?
- Are we building a sustainable business model?
- What are the biggest risks and how do we mitigate them?
- Are we focused on the right priorities?
- How does this decision affect our competitive position?
- What do customers actually want vs. what they say they want?
